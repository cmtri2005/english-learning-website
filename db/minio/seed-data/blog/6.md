
# Master Common English Idioms

> **Idioms** make your English sound more natural and native-like. Here are **20 essential idioms** you’ll hear in everyday conversations.

## Idioms About Time

Use these when talking about deadlines, timing, and schedules:

- **Better late than never** — It’s better to do something late than not at all  
- **In the nick of time** — Just in time, at the last possible moment  
- **Time flies** — Time passes quickly  

**Mini practice:**  
> “I finished the report **in the nick of time**.”



## Idioms About Money

Use these when discussing prices, budgets, and spending:

- **Break the bank** — Cost too much money  
- **Cost an arm and a leg** — Very expensive  
- **On a shoestring budget** — With very little money  

**Mini practice:**  
> “We’re traveling **on a shoestring budget**, so we’ll cook at home.”



## Idioms About Emotions

Use these to describe feelings naturally:

- **Over the moon** — Extremely happy  
- **Under the weather** — Feeling sick  
- **On cloud nine** — Very happy  

**Note:** *Over the moon* and *on cloud nine* are similar, but *over the moon* can feel a bit stronger.



## Quick Tip to Learn Idioms Faster

✅ Don’t memorize the meaning alone—memorize a **full sentence** with it.  
That’s how idioms become natural in your speaking.
