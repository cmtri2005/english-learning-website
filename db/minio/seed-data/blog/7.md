
# American vs British English

> While mutually intelligible, **American** and **British** English have notable differences in **spelling**, **vocabulary**, and **pronunciation**.



## Quick Navigation
- [Spelling Differences](#spelling-differences)
- [Vocabulary Differences](#vocabulary-differences)

## Spelling Differences

| American  | British   |
|-|--|
| color    | colour    |
| center   | centre    |
| analyze  | analyse   |
| traveled | travelled |

**Pattern to notice:**
- **-or** (AmE) ↔ **-our** (BrE)  
- **-er** (AmE) ↔ **-re** (BrE)  
- **-yze** (AmE) ↔ **-yse** (BrE)  
- Single **l** (AmE) ↔ double **ll** in some past forms (BrE)



## Vocabulary Differences

| American   | British |
|--||
| apartment | flat    |
| elevator  | lift    |
| truck     | lorry   |
| cookie    | biscuit |

**Tip:** Vocabulary differences appear most often in daily-life topics (housing, transportation, food).



## Which Should You Learn?

✅ **Both are correct.** Choose based on your goals, location, or preference.

- If you live/study/work in the **US** → lean American  
- If you live/study/work in the **UK/EU** → lean British  
- For exams:
  - **IELTS**: either is fine—just be consistent
  - **TOEFL**: American is more common

> **Best rule:** Pick one as your “default” and keep spelling consistent in writing.
